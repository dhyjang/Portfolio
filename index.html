Module 05 — About

From the classroom to the design studio

I spent six years teaching math in DC Public Schools before moving into instructional design — first at Johns Hopkins and the University of Maryland, now in community college online learning. That classroom experience is the foundation of everything I design: I know what clarity, structure, and access mean to a learner, because I've watched what happens when they're missing.

Today my work sits where learning science, accessibility, and technology meet: reviewing courses against national quality standards, building automations that give educators their time back, leading digital accessibility from audit to remediation, and helping institutions think through responsible AI. Whatever the project, the goal is the same — learning experiences that work for everyone, backed by systems that keep working after I've moved on.
